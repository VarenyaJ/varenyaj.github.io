? Berlin Guide: From Adina Hotel Checkpoint Charlie

You're staying at Adina Apartment Hotel Berlin Checkpoint Charlie in Mitte. Below is a list of sightseeing spots, vegan/vegetarian dining options, and other activities, organized by distance from the hotel, complete with transit instructions.

  • Nearby Stations:
    • U-Bahn: U Stadtmitte (U2, U6) - ~5 min walk
    • Bus: M29, 200 (stops "Checkpoint Charlie" / "Friedrichstrasse")

??? 10 - 20 Min Walk / 1-2 Stops Transit

1. Museum Island (Museumsinsel)

What: UNESCO World Heritage site with five major museums (Pergamon, Altes Museum, Bode, Neues Museum, Alte Nationalgalerie).
Walk: ~ 17 min (1.3 km).
Transit:

  • Option A: From U Stadtmitte, take U6 -> "Friedrichstrasse," then walk ~ 5 min to Museumsinsel.
  • Option B: Bus 200 or M29 -> "Spandauer Str./Museumsinsel."

2. Berlinische Galerie

What: Museum of modern art (20th - 21st century), photography, architecture.
Walk: ~ 15 min (1.2 km).
Transit: Bus M29 -> "Kochstrasse," then walk ~ 3 min.

3. Humboldt Forum

What: New cultural center in the reconstructed Berlin Palace (exhibitions, lecture halls, restaurants).
Walk: ~ 18 min (1.4 km).
Transit:

  • From U Stadtmitte, take U6 -> "Museumsinsel" (1 stop), then walk ~ 5 min.

4. Savory Chay (Vegan Vietnamese)

What: Highly-rated vegan pho, noodle bowls, and other Vietnamese specialties.
Walk: ~ 18 min (1.4 km).
Transit: From U Stadtmitte, take U6 -> "Oranienburger Tor" (2 stops), then walk ~ 5 min.

? 20 - 30 Min Walk / Short Ride

1. Boat Tours on the Spree

What: Various boat operators depart near Museumsinsel or Friedrichstrasse piers. A scenic cruise along the Spree shows the Reichstag, Museumsinsel, Berlin Cathedral, government district.
Walk: ~ 20-25 min to the piers at "Haus der Kulturen der Welt" or "Monbijoubrucke."
Transit: Take U6 or Bus 100/200 -> "Friedrichstrasse" -> then walk ~ 5 min to "Haus der Kulturen der Welt."

2. Soy (Vegan Asian Fusion)

What: Trendy vegan-Asian fusion spot in Mitte: bowls, dumplings, sushi.
Transit: From U Stadtmitte, take U2 -> "Rosa-Luxemburg-Platz" (1 stop), then walk ~ 4 min.

3. Martin-Gropius-Bau

What: Historic 19th-century exhibition hall on Niederkirchnerstrasse (hosting rotating art/cultural exhibitions).
Transit: Bus M29 from "Friedrichstrasse/Checkpoint Charlie" -> "Anhalter Bahnhof" (2 stops), then walk ~ 3 min.

? Short Transit Ride (2-4 Stops)

1. Kurfurstendamm (Shopping Boulevard)

What: Major shopping avenue--boutiques, cafes, department stores (KaDeWe at Wittenbergplatz).
Transit:

  1. From U Stadtmitte, take U6 -> transfer at "Stadtmitte" to U2 -> "Zoologischer Garten" (3 stops total), then walk to Ku'damm.
  2. OR: Take Bus M29 -> "Kurfurstendamm" (~ 10 stops via "Wittenbergstrasse").

2. Berlin State Opera (Staatsoper Unter den Linden)

What: Historic baroque opera house on Unter den Linden.
Transit: From U Stadtmitte, take U6 -> "Franzosische Strasse" (1 stop), then walk ~ 4 min.

3. Mauerweg (Berlin Wall Trail)

What: Former Berlin Wall path--great for biking or running. Nearest section is around Friedrichshain. You can rent bikes or e-scooters (Lime, Tier).
Transit: Walk ~ 5 min to the nearest dock, rent a bike/e-scooter, and follow the trail.

? English <-> German Phrasebook for a Vegetarian in Berlin

You are a 23-year-old American vegetarian (no meat, eggs, fish; dairy OK). You'll be in Berlin for six months. Below is an extensive list of phrases (English ? German), with pronunciations and expected answers, to help you navigate life in Germany as a vegetarian.

GENERAL GREETINGS & POLITE PHRASES

  1. Hello.
    German: Hallo.
    Pronunciation: HAH-loh
    Expected answer:
    • "Hallo!" (HAH-loh) - "Hello!"
  2. Good morning.
    German: Guten Morgen.
    Pronunciation: GOO-ten MOR-gen
    Expected answer:
    • "Guten Morgen!" (GOO-ten MOR-gen) - "Good morning!"
  3. Good evening.
    German: Guten Abend.
    Pronunciation: GOO-ten AH-bend
    Expected answer:
    • "Guten Abend!" (GOO-ten AH-bend) - "Good evening!"
  4. Goodbye.
    German: Auf Wiedersehen.
    Pronunciation: owf VEE-der-zayn
    Expected answer:
    • "Auf Wiedersehen!" (owf VEE-der-zayn) - "Goodbye!"
  5. Please.
    German: Bitte.
    Pronunciation: BIT-uh
    Expected answer:
    • "Bitte." (BIT-uh) - "Please." (also means "You're welcome.")
  6. Thank you.
    German: Danke.
    Pronunciation: DAHN-kuh
    Expected answer:
    • "Bitte." (BIT-uh) - "You're welcome."
    • "Bitte sehr." (BIT-uh ZAY-er) - "You're very welcome."
  7. You're welcome.
    German: Gern geschehen.
    Pronunciation: GAIRN guh-SHAY-en
    Expected answer:
    • "Gern geschehen." (GAIRN guh-SHAY-en) - "You're welcome."
  8. Excuse me / Sorry.
    German: Entschuldigung.
    Pronunciation: ent-SHOOL-dee-goong
    Expected answer:
    • "Kein Problem." (KINE proh-BLAYM) - "No problem."

  9. I don't speak German.
    German: Ich spreche kein Deutsch.
    Pronunciation: ish SHPREH-khuh kine DOYCH

    Pronunciation: SHPREH-khen zee ENG-lish?
    Expected answer:
    • "Ja, ein bisschen." (YAH, ine BISH-hen) - "Yes, a little."

  11. I am a vegetarian.
    German: Ich bin Vegetarier.
    Pronunciation: ish bin veh-guh-TAH-ree-er
    Expected answer:

  12. I do not eat meat.
    German: Ich esse kein Fleisch.
    Pronunciation: ish ESS-uh kine FLYESH
    Expected answer:
    • "Okay, wir haben vegane Gerichte." (OH-kay, veer HAH-ben vay-GAH-nuh guh-RIKHT-uh) - "Okay, we have vegan dishes."
  13. I do not eat fish.
    German: Ich esse keinen Fisch.
    Pronunciation: ish ESS-uh KINE-en fish
    Expected answer:
    • "Verstanden, kein Fisch." (fer-SHTAN-den, kine fish) - "Understood, no fish."
  14. I do not eat eggs.
    German: Ich esse keine Eier.
    Pronunciation: ish ESS-uh KINE-uh EYE-er
    Expected answer:
    • "Alles klar, ohne Eier." (AHL-les klahr, OH-n? EYE-er) - "All clear, without eggs."
  15. Is this vegetarian?
    German: Ist das vegetarisch?
    Pronunciation: ist dahs veh-guh-TAH-rish?
    Expected answer:
    • "Ja, es ist vegetarisch." (YAH, es ist veh-guh-TAH-rish) - "Yes, it is vegetarian."
